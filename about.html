Our ideals

When was the last time you purchased a shirt and thought about the hands that made it and the journey it took to get to you? Chances are high that something you own was made in Bangladesh, the world’s second largest exporter of clothing. However, most of the time very little information is available about the conditions in which that item was made.

We’re launching a brand called New Market Goods that highlights positive approaches to garment production. We’ve partnered with Deshal, a community of local garment producers who’ve been striving to create better working conditions in their factory in Bangladesh. Together we hope to promote good design, celebrate Bangladeshi artisanship, and improve transparency in the production of clothing, starting with our line of popover shirts.

Our mission

1) Create beautiful garments that reflect the heritage of their origin, but add vibrancy where it's dearly needed. Deshal was already doing this; we’re just making adaptations to fit a new context.

2) Increase transparency of an industry that until recently has only been featured for the extremely negative working conditions. For us, transparency starts with knowing the journey that every one of our shirts takes.

3) Improve local livelihoods by returning a larger share directly back to the community contributing to the project. I heard a quote from Jacqueline Novogratz of the Acumen Fund on TED Radio Hour a few weeks ago that we should aspire to reflect in our own efforts in livelihood improvement: “It’s about giving dignity. Believing that you have a chance as a human being to be a greater part in determining your own destiny.”
